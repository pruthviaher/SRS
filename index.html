Shri Ram Srushti

Our Story

Welcome to the sacred land of Chas (Nali), where the echoes of an ancient legend resonate with every ripple in the river Godavari. Our village holds a timeless connection to the divine, woven into the epic journey of Lord Ram.

It is said that during their time in the forest, Goddess Sita was captivated by the sight of a golden deer. Wishing to fulfill her desire, Lord Ram set off to chase the mystical creature through the dense forest. As the golden deer led him further away, Lord Ram, with his unparalleled skill, unleashed his divine arrows to subdue the elusive being.

One of these powerful arrows, infused with Lord Ram's divine energy, is believed to have struck the Godavari River. The impact of this sacred shot created a deep, rocky crack in the riverbed, forming a natural boundary known as the "Nali." This crack has stood the test of time, and legend has it that the very arrow shot by Lord Ram still rests deep within this fissure. However, the depth of the crack and the rugged terrain have made it impossible to retrieve, keeping this divine relic hidden within the heart of the river.

Our village, named after this divine event, bears testimony to the presence of Lord Ram and his sacred influence on our land. The new Ram Mandir stands as a beacon of faith, a place where the past and present come together to honor the legacy of Lord Ram’s journey.

As we gather for the inauguration of this temple, we celebrate not only the divine story of Lord Ram but also the sacred bond that ties our village to this epic moment. Welcome to Chas (Nali), where faith, history, and divinity come together in harmony.
